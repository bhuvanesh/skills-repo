# Week 1 — Foundation Homework

This week is about giving Claude (and yourself) enough context to make decisions about your business. Once these files are filled in, every future answer from Claude becomes specific to *your* business instead of generic advice.

## What you should expect

- **Time budget:** 3–5 hours total, split across 2–3 sessions. Don't try to do it in one sitting — you'll get sloppy.
- **Quality > completeness.** Better to write "unknown" on a field and move on than to fake an answer. Come back next week.
- **First pass only.** These files are living documents. You'll edit them every month or two.

---

## The files (in the order you should fill them)

1. **`00-START-HERE.md`** — *~5 min.* Orientation. Just read it.
2. **`BUSINESS-CONTEXT.md`** — *~20–30 min.* The single most important file. Claude reads it on every request.
3. **`CLAUDE.md`** — *~10 min.* How you want Claude to talk to you. Light personalisation.
4. **`01-STRATEGY/BUSINESS-MODEL.md`** — *~30–45 min.* How the business actually makes money.
5. **`01-STRATEGY/CUSTOMER-PROFILES.md`** — *~45–60 min.* Usually the biggest one. Worth the time.
6. **`01-STRATEGY/POSITIONING.md`** — *~30–45 min.* Easier to do *after* customer profiles, not before.
7. **`01-STRATEGY/COMPETITOR-NOTES.md`** — *~30 min.* Include indirect competitors (DIY, doing nothing, software).
8. **`01-STRATEGY/90-DAY-PRIORITIES.md`** — *~30 min.* Do this last — it builds on everything above.

---

## How to fill each file

Two ways. Use whichever fits the moment.

**Option A — Guided by Claude (recommended for first pass):**

```text
/onboarding-help BUSINESS-CONTEXT.md
```

Claude reads the file, walks you through it section by section, and writes your answers back in. For open-ended fields where you might stare at a blank line ("Hidden pains", "Things to stop doing"), Claude will offer 3–5 directional thought starters drawn from common business patterns — not recommendations, just angles to react to. The first time it does this, it'll check whether the starters are helping. If they're slowing you down, say so and it'll switch to plain questions for the rest of the session.

**Option B — Solo in your editor:**

Open the file in your editor and type after each `Label:` line. Tables and bullet lists you fill in normally. Skip anything you don't know — write `unknown` or leave it blank. Move on.

---

## What "done" looks like for Week 1

You don't need every Label filled. You need *enough* that:

- Claude can describe your business in one sentence without inventing details.
- You can run `/onboarding-help 01-STRATEGY/90-DAY-PRIORITIES.md` and the three priorities you write down actually feel like the right ones — not random ideas.
- A new team member could read `BUSINESS-CONTEXT.md` and have a working mental model of the business in 10 minutes.

If those three are true, you're done with Week 1.

---

## A few rules to keep things simple

- **One file at a time.** Don't jump around.
- **Short answers beat long ones.** One or two sentences per Label is usually enough.
- **Capture customer language verbatim** in `CUSTOMER-PROFILES.md`. Don't paraphrase.
- **`unknown` is a valid answer.** Mark it and move on.
- **Re-read each file once before closing it.** Catch the obvious gaps yourself.
